Meowdoku Solver

Tactics guide
The ground rules Each row, column, and color gets exactly one cat. Cats can't touch — not even diagonally, so all 8 cells around a cat are blocked too.
🎯 Last Spot If a row, column, or color is down to exactly one open cell, the cat must go there.
📏 Line Lock If all of a color's open cells sit in one line, that color owns the line — cross every other color out of it. Works in reverse too: if all of a line's open cells are one color, that color's cat is in this line — cross that color out everywhere else.
👥 Crowding If K colors can only fit in the same K lines, those lines are fully booked — cross every other color out of them. (K=2 is the classic "naked pair".) Works in reverse too: if K lines can only be reached by K colors combined, those colors must place inside those lines — cross their other cells out everywhere else.
🤏 Shared Shadow Look at ALL remaining candidates for a row, column, or color. If every single one of them would eliminate a certain cell — through its row, column, color, or by touching it — that cell is dead no matter which candidate wins.
🤝 Team Shadow Sometimes no single color or line dooms a cell alone, but two of them together do — every possible way their two cats could land still eliminates the same cell.
🤔 What-If Test a cell: pretend a cat sits there and follow every move that becomes forced. If some row, column, or color ends up with nowhere to go, that cell was impossible — cross it out. Shown one dead end at a time with its forced chain drawn out, so it stays followable; use Apply All to clear every dead end found at once.
